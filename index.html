From RF Harvesting to Robotic Factory — What Actually Works

Physics boundary: CMB power density is ~0.1 pW/m² — ten billion times weaker than urban WiFi. CMB cannot power anything. Harvestable RF comes exclusively from human-made transmitters. Vacuum fluctuations cannot be harvested without violating thermodynamics (no Casimir engine).
What works: A 100 cm² metamaterial patch in urban RF captures ~10 μW after rectification. Combined with thermoelectric (80 μW) and piezo (50 μW), total body harvest reaches ~140 μW — enough for sensor hub + BLE beacon.

E.2 Schottky Rectification (The Spark Gap)

Converting RF to DC requires a rectifying junction. The contact between gold shell and magnetite core creates a Schottky barrier — a microscopic one-way gate for current.

I = Is · [exp(qV / nkBT) - 1] Schottky diode equation

The barrier height φB determines the minimum voltage for rectification:

JunctionφB (eV)Ideality nApplication
Au / n-Si0.801.05Standard Schottky, proven
Au / Fe3O40.451.8Black sand heterojunction
Au / GaAs0.901.02High-frequency rectenna
Au / Graphene0.351.3Ultra-low barrier, flexible
🧪 The Gold-Magnetite Heterojunction ▶

The Au/Fe3O4 interface creates a natural Schottky diode. Magnetite (Fe3O4) is a half-metal — it conducts like a metal for one spin direction and insulates for the other. This spin-selective transport is real and well-documented.

The gold shell provides:

  • Chemical inertness (biocompatible)
  • High surface conductivity for antenna function
  • The Schottky contact for rectification

The 0.45 eV barrier means it needs ~200 mV to turn on. For ambient RF (10-100 mV), you need arrays in series or tunnel-enhanced junctions to reach the threshold.

E.4 Stochastic Resonance

Counter-intuitive: noise can enhance signal detection. In a nonlinear bistable system, sub-threshold signals become detectable when the right amount of noise is added.

U(x) = -ax²/2 + bx⁴/4 Double-well potential (bistable system)
rKramers = (a√b / 2π) · exp(-ΔU / D) Kramers escape rate (D = noise intensity)
Engineering application: The granular Au/Fe3O4 film is inherently a bistable nonlinear system. Each grain-to-grain contact is a potential well. Environmental RF noise can push the system over the barrier, causing coherent switching — signal extraction from apparent chaos. This is real physics, demonstrated experimentally in superconducting junctions, neural systems, and electronic circuits.
🧠 Why this matters for the wearable ▶

Traditional rectenna design tries to eliminate noise. But if the Au/Fe3O4 film operates in the stochastic resonance regime, it can harvest signals that are below the classical rectification threshold.

The optimal noise level depends on the barrier height of the Schottky junctions. For φB = 0.45 eV, the optimal thermal noise is near body temperature (kBT ≈ 27 meV at 310K). This is not coincidence — body heat provides the noise, ambient RF provides the signal.

However: the total harvestable power is still bounded by the incident power density. Stochastic resonance improves detection, not energy extraction beyond what arrives.

E.6 The Robotics Factory Path

Building self-sustaining systems requires phased escalation. Each tier must work before the next.

🏭 Energy Tiers: Factory → Wearable ▶

Tier 0 — Factory: Grid power, 100W-10kW per robot. Full CPU/GPU compute. Ethernet/WiFi. CNC + 3D printing + pick-and-place.

Tier 1 — Mobile: Battery, 10-100W. Edge AI (Cortex A53 + NPU). WiFi/5G. Autonomous navigation, manipulation.

Tier 2 — Wearable: Solar+battery, 50-500 mW. Microcontroller (M4/RISC-V). BLE 5.3. On-body sensors, local inference.

Tier 3 — Harvest: Body harvest only, 50-200 μW. Sensor hub (M0+). Backscatter comms. Sleep-dominant operation.

🤖 Self-Replicating Factory Phases ▶

Phase 1: Human-operated CNC + 3D printing. Design files from manifesto repos.

Phase 2: Robot-assisted assembly. Human supervision, robot does repetitive pick-and-place.

Phase 3: Robot-manufactured subassemblies. Humans handle integration and QC.

Phase 4: Robot builds robot (supervised). Human approves each unit before activation.

Phase 5: Autonomous expansion. Self-replicating factory with human oversight on output quality.

Each phase requires the previous phase working reliably. No shortcuts. No skipping.

Engineering law: You cannot power a factory from body heat. You cannot skip from "sensor node" to "self-replicating factory" without the intermediate tiers. The wearable IS the sensor layer of a larger system. The factory IS the Tier 0 that builds the Tier 3 devices. They are different tiers of the same architecture, not the same device.
